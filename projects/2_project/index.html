NeuroProfiling:
Spatial Quantification of SNC Neurons

Computational pipeline for quantifying spatial organization of substantia nigra pars compacta (SNC) neurons from histological images.

Hnasko Lab, UC San Diego 2024-2025 Neuroimaging Research

Project Overview

NeuroProfiling is a computational pipeline that quantifies the spatial organization of substantia nigra pars compacta (SNC) neurons from histological images. The primary objective is to measure the shortest distance between each neuron and the ventral (or dorsal) boundary.

The pipeline combines standardized image masks, OpenCV contour detection, spline-based boundary smoothing, and bounded evaluation to compute accurate shortest distances and produce interpretable statistics such as mean, standard deviation, median, and quartiles. This enables quantitative analysis of neuronal spatial distribution patterns in the substantia nigra.

Key Techniques

Image Segmentation

Standardized image tiling and grayscale conversion with cell masks (Aldh1a1⁺ and Aldh1a1⁻) scaled to avoid overlap and ensure one cell per tile.

Contour Detection

OpenCV-based extraction of ventral boundary as the largest connected contour, with cell contours reduced to centroids representing neuron locations.

Distance Analysis

Spline interpolation with smoothing factor adjustment and bounded parameter evaluation to compute accurate shortest distances from neurons to boundaries.

Applications & Impact

Neurological Research

Enables quantitative analysis of neuronal spatial distribution patterns in the substantia nigra, crucial for understanding Parkinson's disease and other neurodegenerative conditions.

Statistical Analysis

Provides comprehensive statistical summaries including counts, mean, standard deviation, median, P25, and P75, enabling comparison across images or subjects.

Histological Analysis

Automates the quantification of spatial relationships between neurons and anatomical boundaries, reducing manual analysis time and improving reproducibility.

Computational Pipeline

Provides a standardized, automated approach to neuroimaging analysis that can be applied across multiple subjects and research studies.
